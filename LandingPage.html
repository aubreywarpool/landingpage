SPCA Cincinnati Mobile App

Using app technology to help SPCA promote adoption and animal wellness

Prototype Link

The SPCA Cincinnati Mobile App is an app designed to help promote adoption and fostering of rescued animals. The staff and volunteers of SPCA Cincinnati work hard to provide the best treatment for animals in their care. However, an average of 36 new animals per day calls for increased community involvement to save the lives of animals in need. Although the SPCA has a mobile version of their website, I believe that developing an interactive app would increase community awareness and involvement, and promote adoption, fostering, and donations for the SPCA Cincinnati.

#4. Three main features (menu, information architecture)

Notifications

User Profiles

Animal Livestream
A notifications tab would provide users with alerts when new dogs and cats have become available for fostering/adoption, when new volunteer oppotunities have been posted, and when the shelter is in need of donatable items. This page will also link users to portals that would allow them to sign up to volunteer or donate/purchase a needed item for the shelter.
A profile system for users would allow them to post pictures and videos of themselves and their pets, promoting user involvement. The page would also provide the user with an opportunity to link their social media accounts to increase SPCA Cincinnati's exposure.
A live-streaming feature of the adoptable cats in Kitty City is likely to get the animals more exposure and promote adoption, increase traffic to the app, and entertain and involve viewers that simply enjoy watching cats.

"Exposure is the key to increasing adoption.
The more people see these animals, the more likely they are to find forever homes."

Interview with Elle Willmoth, SPCA Employee

Design process

  • What services does the SPCA need help with?
  • How could an app help to meet these needs?
  • How do those needs translate to specific features on the app?

To better understand how technology could benefit the SPCA, I visited the SPCA Cincinnati location in Northside. After speaking to employees and petting cats and dogs, I found that the SPCA is always in need of donations and as much volunteer help as they can get. Their website also currently does not provide an online portal for their regular volunteers to pick up shifts. Another feature that might benefit the shelter would be allowing donations to be purchased in-app.

Insight Statement

Using technology the right way could help with overcrowding and underfunding in animal shelters.

Group Discussion

Although everyone in my group had individual ideas, all of them centered around community and connection with others. We were all looking for ways to connect users and promote sharing of information between them.

  • How might we use technology to encourage discourse between users?
  • How might we use that discourse and website traffic to benefit the SCPA Cincinnati?
  • How might we develop an app with features that encourage community involvement and volunteering?
